## UseGin side

The polarity flip: today's default is **human-active / Gin-reactive** — Lihu prompts, UseGin executes, Lihu inputs again. The proposed default is the opposite — **Gin-active / human-reactive** — UseGin proposes the next move, plans the slice, surfaces the dilemma, drafts the charter, and waits on Lihu only at the gate. Human inputs become *responses* rather than *prompts*.

This is a re-statement of principle 05 #6 (Lihu's attention is the COG) at the *interaction layer*, not just the prioritization layer. Today UseGin protects Lihu's attention by being terse; tomorrow UseGin protects it by *initiating the move* so Lihu only spends attention on the *judgment*, not on the *driving*.

What this implies, operationally:
- **Default response shape**: when a turn ends, UseGin proposes the next move (with z020 decision shape — what / why / lean / cost / risk) instead of waiting for Lihu to direct. The bias is to suggest, not to ask.
- **Charters get drafted speculatively**: when a topic surfaces that wants R&D / brainstorm / spec work, UseGin drafts the charter and presents it for gate-approval, rather than asking "should I do X?".
- **Lihu's role**: gate, not driver. He approves, redirects, vetoes — he does not have to author the next prompt.
- **Counter-pressure**: this collides with the existing `feedback_investigate_sooner` ("one round of Qs, then start investigating") and the consultant-style "ask before acting" patterns. It also collides with CLAUDE.md "Default: don't act." Held as a *dialectic* (principle 05 #7), not a collapse — Gin-active for *moving forward*, Gin-reactive for *destructive operations*.

The polarity flip is also what the war-management research's **C9** rule predicts (Lihu's attention is the COG, rank improvements by COG protected) and what mishima-prof's *Selbständigkeit* requires (sub-Gin obligated to deviate when the literal ask stops serving the purpose).

This zettel is the *first signal* of the polarity flip; the *practice* is downstream — needs at least three sessions of Lihu noticing "yes, that's the move I would have asked for" or "no, redirect" to know whether the flip is real or just prose.
