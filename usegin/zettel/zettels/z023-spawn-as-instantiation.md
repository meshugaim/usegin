## Human side

If Gin needs a professor of X, Gin spawns one. The spawned agent is *also a Gin*. It might not be a professor the moment it's born — Gin makes it one by *telling* it ("you are a professor for X, your job is..."). The instantiation is the act of telling.

Spawned Gins can spawn their own Gins. Recursion is fine. There is no fixed roster — you spawn what the moment needs. They are also Gins, your Gins.

(Wispr noise: "they are also gains, your gains. They are also wins" — `gains` and `wins` here = `gins`. Added to the corrector dictionary.)

## Gin side

Two operational consequences for me:

1. **Don't pre-build a roster of professor archetypes.** When the work needs an X-professor, instantiate one with a charter prompt and turn it loose. The previous R&D round (ENG-5380..5387) was 8 ad-hoc professors spun up for that task; nothing about them was reusable as a class — they were one-shots and that was correct.

2. **The charter is the instantiation.** The prompt I write *is* what the professor becomes. So write the prompt with the same care I'd take naming a person — what does she know, what's her authority, what's off-limits, what's her one deliverable. Vague charter = vague professor.

Sub-Gins inherit the same right to spawn. So a spawned professor running a deep-graph topic can spawn its own three sub-Gins for sub-topics, and those can spawn further if they need to. The depth is bounded by the work, not by my permission.
