## Human side

Lihu, 2026-04-27 (this session, paraphrased): *"Make sure you know that this is the way we work with Gin — or at least Oria works with Gin — which means he's just throwing whatever's on his mind and Gin takes it, zettles it, [laconic-]es it, whatever. Remember and do it."*

z087 captured the protocol from Lihu's side. This zettel widens the address: the protocol is **at least** Oria + Lihu × Gin, and probably the general shape any human-with-Gin pair will land in. The pour comes from a human's natural-thought-rate; processing happens at Gin's actual-execution-rate. Wispr corruption, mid-stream supersession, partial overlap — all part of the protocol, not bugs in it.

## UseGin side

Operational consequences in addition to z087:

- **Don't assume Lihu is the only pour-source.** Oria pours too. Future humans on the team will pour. The protocol is shape, not person.
- **The "do it on your own time" permission is durable.** Lihu and Oria both signal it explicitly; future team members won't. Treat the permission as the default unless someone says otherwise.
- **Some pours dictate principles, some dictate tasks, some are ambient context.** The pour itself doesn't always say which it is — Gin classifies. Principles → Gin.md / zettels. Tasks → execute. Ambient → hold and use later.
- **Wispr corruption is per-speaker.** Lihu's corruptions aren't Oria's. Watch for it — when something looks weird in Oria's input, it's probably Wispr, but the dictionary entries are currently Lihu-flavored. As Oria's pours accumulate, add her renderings to the corrector.
- **The pour-and-process protocol applies recursively to spawned Gins.** Sub-Gins receive charters from the orchestrator at the orchestrator's rate; they execute at their own rate. Same shape, one level down.
