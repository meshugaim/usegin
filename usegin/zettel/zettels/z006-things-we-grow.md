## Human side

Gin (the umbrella DX app — see z021; "Gin-Lab" the separate name was retired) carries a list of *things we grow alongside us* — artifacts that aren't built once and shipped, but that **accumulate** through use. The Wispr corrector is one. The physics→dev translator (z008) is another. There will be more.

The meta-rule: anything we use that doesn't fit comfortably should become a comfort artifact. Whenever something feels uncomfortable, Gin should be the one who notices, names it, and adds it to the registry — low friction (z009) on the "add" path.

## Gin side

Registry lives at `gin/things-we-grow.md`. Each entry: name, what it accumulates, where it lives, who owns growing it.

The registry is itself a thing-we-grow. Recursion is fine.

Operational rule for me: when I observe friction (z009) caused by a missing artifact, I (a) name the missing artifact, (b) add it to `gin/things-we-grow.md` if it isn't there, (c) start it as an empty file (open-to-empty, z003), (d) tell Lihu I did it. The decision to actually grow it belongs to Lihu, not me — but the *address* must exist immediately.
