## 1. "Distillation" — Explicitly Named as a Product Feature Concept

**Source:** Team meeting notes (Fathom, shared internally, ~early April 2026 — the same email appears in at least 4 threads)

The concept was explicitly labeled **"Distilled Context Feature Concept"** in team meeting summaries:

> **"Inspiration:** A user's Claude workflow for synthesizing career history."
>
> **Workflow:**
> 1. **Raw Input:** Uploaded unedited, stream-of-consciousness notes.
> 2. **AI Synthesis:** Claude, with human guidance, **distilled the raw data into structured markdown documents.**
> 3. **Application:** These distilled documents were then used as the core context for new tasks (e.g., analyzing job descriptions), leading to more consistent and accurate results.
>
> **Potential for Effie:**
> - **Benefit:** Using distilled context could **significantly improve AI efficiency and consistency** compared to processing raw data for every query.
> - **Mechanism:** Effie could **proactively ask clarifying questions** to help users create these distilled summaries, which would then become part of the **project's persistent context.**

The same meeting lists it as priority item #3 after SharePoint integration and latency reduction. Guy's action item: *"Refine the proactive features concept into a detailed spec."*

## 5. Decision Documentation as Organizational Memory — "The New Physics" Document

**Source:** Attachment — *"The New Physics of Project Intelligence"* (author unnamed, circulating internally)

This is the richest single source on capturing decisions AND the WHY. Verbatim:

> **"Use Case 6: Decision Documentation — Collecting Memo as Memory"**
>
> "The old physics: Decisions are made in meetings. Rationale is partially captured in notes, partially lost. **When stakeholders rotate, context is lost and decisions get relitigated.**
>
> The new physics: After any significant decision, the system prompts key participants (in parallel): **'What was your understanding of why we made this decision? What tradeoffs did we accept?'** Synthesizes into a **'decision record' that captures multiple perspectives on the rationale — not a sanitized single narrative.**
>
> Snowden connection: This directly addresses capability atrophy risk. Instead of the system 'carrying context' in a way that lets humans forget, it captures **human sense-making at the moment of decision — preserving the reasoning rather than just the outcome. It's spaced repetition for organizational memory.**"

> **"Use Case 5: Cross-Project Weak Signal Detection"**
>
> "The most valuable patterns are often emergent across projects but **not named by anyone, present in the negative space** (what's conspicuously absent across multiple contexts), or **contradictions between what different teams believe to be true.**"
>
> "The meeting notes say 'alignment reached.' But the system notices: **two participants' typed contributions during the meeting expressed reservations they never vocalized** ... The system surfaces: 'The meeting concluded with stated alignment on X, but contribution patterns suggest this alignment may be **shallow.**'"

## 6. Associative / "Pull a Wire" Retrieval — The Zettelkasten Concept

The exact phrase "pull a wire" wasn't found verbatim, but the concept was extensively discussed under the **Zettelkasten** label — multiple team meetings.

**Source:** Multiple Fathom meeting summaries referencing a team discussion (shared in several email threads, consistent content)

> **"Solution: 'Zettelkasten' Context Graph"**
>
> - **Concept:** An **emergent knowledge graph that connects ideas based on their content, not a pre-defined structure.**
> - **Process:**
>   1. Ingest data (e.g., email, transcript).
>   2. **Extract its essence (summarize, tag).**
>   3. **Map it to the existing graph, connecting to related nodes.**
> - **Key Feature: Time-Awareness** — The graph **tracks how ideas and policies evolve over time.**
> - **Example Query:** "How did our policy on X change from Q3 to Q4?"
> - **Value:** Enables proactive guidance by detecting **shifts in team sentiment** (e.g., from 80/20 confidence in option A to 51/49 against it) before a hard pivot is necessary.

**Source:** Meeting transcript — MoVi Check-in: Pablo & Guy, Dec 5, 2025

> **[00:06:00] Collective Team:** "It's like, what are those decision points, inflection points that happen in a company and **why they happen? What were the conditions that they happened under?**"
>
> **[00:06:45] Collective Team:** "We were more sort of **knowledge graph obsessed.** So, our mission was to build, like, an intuitive knowledge management solution that was, like, knowledge graph-based, where... you could **semantically search across** ... **building like a genetic memory of a team or an institution** and be able to like query across that."
>
> **[00:07:54] Collective Team:** "We sort of built this like knowledge graph software where users can build out topic-specific context windows, essentially using embeddings. And like it **automatically builds out the edges between those nodes** that you upload."
