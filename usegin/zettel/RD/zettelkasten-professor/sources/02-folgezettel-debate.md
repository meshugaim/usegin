# The Folgezettel debate — what threading actually does

There is a real disagreement in the modern community on whether Folgezettel
(positional sequence numbering like 1a, 1a1, 1b) carries cognitive value
beyond what links provide. Both sides are worth knowing.

## Position A — Folgezettel was just a paper workaround (Sascha, zettelkasten.de)

> "although Folgezettel were indeed a very important factor for Luhmann's
> success, this is mostly due to the physical nature of his archive."

In digital systems, direct linking accomplishes what Folgezettel achieved on
paper. Continuing to use Folgezettel digitally is "clutter without cognitive
benefit — treating techniques as more important than underlying principles."

A Folgezettel relation is essentially a categorical hierarchy: "one and only
one connection to the level above." Tags + links subsume it.

## Position B — Folgezettel is a generative discipline (Bob Doto)

Folgezettel forces "**eufriction**" — beneficial friction. When you place a
new note, you must situate it "thematically and visually among others",
which "pushes the note maker toward making at least one connection at the
time of import."

Crucially, the cognitive value is at the **threshold** — *before* you dive
into reading any single note:
> "you can see top-level connections between all your notes without having
> to pull out a single one"

This "bird's eye view" comes for free as a byproduct of the alphanumeric
sequence. Tags don't give it. Links don't give it.

The act of choosing "this note follows [1H2c1a], so it becomes [1H2c1b]" is
itself an act of meaning-making — a forced moment of "where does this
*belong*?" — and that is what makes the box dense rather than sparse.

Doto: "finding myself in my stack day after day has given me an intimate
understanding of my notes as a whole" — a "paper trail of meaning" that
records how each idea was *first contextualized*.

## Resolution for our purposes

Both can be true:
- The exact `1a/1a1` notation is a paper artifact.
- The discipline of *committing each new note to a place inside the existing
  fabric* — and the bird's-eye-view that emerges from that commitment — is
  load-bearing and worth preserving in any digital adaptation.

The mistake is using "we have links now" as license to skip the placement
step. Links without forced placement → flat, sparse soup. Links + forced
placement → the box becomes a partner that surprises you.
