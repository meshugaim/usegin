# Principle 3 — Don't dive into Claude's world; pull Claude into ours

We should not shove our heads into Claude's mess — into the code, into the
architecture, into the implementation churn. That is Claude's domain.

Our domain is the *management world*: deciding what to build, why, when,
and judging whether what came back is good. The 2nd brain serves *that*
layer.

So: pull Claude into our decision-making world, instead of us reaching down
into Claude's executing world. Zettels should mostly capture the meta layer
— intent, rationale, lessons, frustrations, decisions — not the
implementation details (those live in the code and in `git log`).
