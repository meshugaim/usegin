# Principle 4 — Fighting Claude vs. asking Claude

A standing self-check: am I *fighting* Claude right now, or am I *asking*
Claude?

Fighting means tense, repetitive, frustrated, trying to force an outcome
through the agent against the grain. Asking means relaxed, curious, posing
the question and letting the answer come back.

Keep the brain working. Stay relaxed. When the session shifts into
fighting, that itself is a zettel — capture it and step back.

The 2nd brain should help us notice this shift early (clusters of
frustration zettels in an area = we're fighting that area) and treat it as
a signal, not noise.
