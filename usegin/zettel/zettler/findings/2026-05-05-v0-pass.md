## Lihu side

### TL;DR

- **Shape.** 114 zettels on disk (id z082 missing), all created over a 4-day burst (Apr 27–30, 2026); 91% land on Apr 27 alone — this is one big inhale, not steady accrual. Authorship is 70 UseGin / 23 human / 8 legacy "gin" / 3 Zisser / 3 doc-team / 2 orchestrator / 1 post-tikur / 1 consultant.

There is no `dx zettel search`, no auto-pop, no inbound-thread view, and the corpus has 5 broken thread refs already. At ~115 zettels we are below the threshold where anyone can find anything cold.

### Infra audit

**(1) Does `dx zettel search` exist?** **No.** Verified 2026-05-05. Listing of `tools/dx/src/zettel/commands/`:

```
add.ts  it.test.ts  it.ts  link.test.ts  link.ts  list.ts  show.ts
```

Six commands. No `search.ts`. The state z065 documented on 2026-04-27 is unchanged 8 days later. The proposed slice-1.5 stopgap (~10 lines, literal substring match wrapped around `rg`) has not landed; neither has the slice-2 pgvector path.

**(2) Frontmatter consistency.** Sample of 20 + grep over all 114:

- `created` field missing on z094, z095 (have date-line in body instead — pre-rename shape).
- `type` field missing on z094, z095 (same).
- `authored-by` missing on z091, z094, z095 (z091 uses no author field; z094/z095 are old shape).
- `session` field missing on most pre-z033 hand-written zettels and on z091.
- `threads` field uses three forms in the wild:
  - canonical: `threads: [↑z028, ~z020, ~z022]` (most files)
  - bare numeric/short: `threads: [↑22]` (z046), `[↑z22]` (z047) — *these are deliberate test fixtures, not drift*; z060 documents the bug they test.
  - body-style threading footer: `threads: [feedback_be_laconic, ...]` mixing memory-file handles with zettel ids (z106, z091, z098, z099, z110, z111, z113, z114) — these treat threads as free-form labels.
- Older zettels use `## Human side` / `## Gin side` body structure; z033+ uses `## Human side` / `## UseGin side` (rename residue, harmless).
- z032 namespace was contested: gaps.md notes "Two files claim id z032" — verified, z036 frontmatter still has `renamed-from: z032`, leaving auditing tools with a stale duplicate signal.

**Verdict:** schema drift is real but bounded. The 5–8 outliers are all from before the convention firmed up, plus the late-Apr Zisser-authored ones that imported a different threads-as-tags style. No breakage; future readers should know `threads:` can be canonical (`↑zNNN`/`~zNNN`) or free-text-like.

**Outbound vs inbound visibility.** `dx zettel show` shows only outbound threads. The inbound view (z069 — "half the graph is invisible") is a high-cost gap because clusters per z040 *are* the inbound set. Inbound info is recoverable today only via `rg "zNNN" usegin/zettel/zettels/`.

**(4) Storage / database shape.** Markdown-on-disk + git, full stop. There is no Supabase table, no pgvector index, no SQLite cache. Slice 2 was designed (`SLICE-2.md`, `EFFI-SYNC-DESIGN.md`, deviations recorded in z050) but not implemented; the embedding model dilemma in z053 is unresolved.

The non-sql-poc at `experiments/poc-knowledge-store/` recently landed verdict=Iterate, exploring markdown-on-disk + LanceDB as a substrate. The architectural connection is direct: zettels are *already* markdown-on-disk; if the LanceDB experiment iterates to a usable retrieval layer, plugging zettels into it is a one-step move. Worth a separate dispatch — Zettler V0 doesn't propose V1 per charter.

### Gaps (consumption side, unbuilt)

Triangulating `gaps.md` (4 entries, all 2026-04-27, none resolved), the principles, and what the corpus is missing given its stated intent:

- **No retrieval surface.** The README's promise — *"Pulling one wire reveals the whole rope"* — has no implementation. `dx zettel list | grep` and `rg` are the actual recall paths. At 114 zettels we are below the threshold where someone can find what they need cold; at 200+ this becomes the failure mode.
- **No auto-pop.** AUTO-POP-DESIGN.md exists with a thoughtful design (pop-log, reason-chain, frustration-cluster carve-out — z055/z056/z057). No code. Slice 3 is paper.
- **No inbound-thread view.** Stopgap is 5 lines; not landed.
- **No cluster-density tooling.** z040 says clusters are observed, not imposed — but nothing currently observes them. The cluster table at the top of this report had to be hand-counted from the threading graph.
- **No backfill of prior session knowledge.** z084 names this — once `dx zettel add` is robust enough, backfill old high-signal claims with their original dates. Not started.
- **gaps.md has 4 unresolved entries from Apr 27.** Duplicate `id: z032` in front-matter; non-uniform front-matter format; `version:` field never used anywhere; the dx-zettel-add race threading. None resolved in 8 days.
- **No visibility into who's read what.** "Pulling a wire reveals the rope" assumes someone is at the other end pulling. There's no telemetry on which zettels get read, by whom, when.
- **The corpus has stopped accruing.** Last new zettel: 2026-04-30 (z115). 5 days of silence. Either the team got disciplined-elsewhere, or the friction surfaced through May 5 didn't reach the zettel surface (e.g. all the Slack/CASA/sellback work in `zisser/dispatched/` since May 1). Worth checking whether `zettleit` is being used or whether the producing-side friction (z072 — title required up front) is choking capture.

### Consuming-side reality check

Lihu's intent: *"pull a wire here and find what's connected over there."*

- A fresh team member opening this corpus today gets: a list of 114 markdown files in alphabetical-by-id order (`dx zettel list`), no search, no cluster view, no inbound-thread view. They can read top-down or grep their way around.
- The graph is good (1 real broken thread out of ~400 placement+cross edges). The hubs are clear (z022 with 26 inbound; z038 with 17; z028 with 15). But the only way to *see* hubs is to re-do this report's inbound-count grep.
- The producing side did its job: 114 atomic, mostly-distilled, mostly-threaded zettels in 4 days is real volume of real value. The consuming side is where the unit-economics shift requires actual tooling.
