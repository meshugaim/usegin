# Customer outcomes

End-user-level usage. What named humans actually did with Effi, what they got, whether it stuck. Distinct from [design-partners](design-partners.md), which tracks the *partner* relationship; here we track *individual sessions, queries, and observable result*. Internal AskEffi-team dogfooding is included — Guy is the most prolific Effi user indexed.

#### 3. Phil Lau (Urban Insight) — independent solo session, ~Mar 17–22

The single clearest external end-user case of unaided querying on real project data, with written feedback delivered back.

- Loaded a real project; asked: *"Where are we in the project?"* → got factual answer including that a kickoff scheduled for Mar 4 hadn't happened.
- Follow-up: *"It's Mar 20 now, are we at risk if the kickoff hasn't happened?"* → got risk assessment, but only when asked directly.
- Asked for other risks → *"found and factually returned"*.
- Asked for undocumented risks Effi foresees → *"gave good output"*.
- Wrote unsolicited feedback: *"Effi is very factual. The system is good and going through the artifacts to pull up details I've requested."* The gap he flagged: wanted **proactive** synthesis — *"tell me there could be risk because there's no documentation of a Mar 4 kickoff having happened"*, rather than only on-demand. — gmail:be475495.
- **Bug surfaced:** chat history wiped between sessions. Phil tested Thursday, returned Sunday to write feedback, found *"a clean slate"*. — gmail:ebfb57cd.
- Internal Fathom note (later): *"Urban Insight: A previous rejection, but the contact remains an active user."*

**Result:** ✅ Genuine repeated use. The accuracy bar was met; the proactivity bar was not. Persistence-of-history was a then-bug.
