### Sales process

Documented step sequence (Fathom summary fathom.video/calls/512444627):

> **Pitch → Activate free account → Secure payment for paid plan → Enable self-service upgrades in UI**

Other framing:
- **Discovery > sales calls in current phase:** *"I want to optimize for discovery calls over sales calls since we are still in validation mode"* — gmail:2f231d1e.
- **Demo:** live product demo with or without live data; synthetic SAP-implementation example available for prospects who can't share their own.
- **Design partnership** = formalised pre-paid stage. DPA + design partner agreement via DocuSign. Free early access in exchange for feedback + word-of-mouth.
- **Activation call:** structured onboarding (product walkthrough, set up test account, integration requirements, implementation path) — gmail:3a43a9a9.
- **Free → paid transition:** *"The $500/mo design partnership is a low-friction entry point, not a long-term revenue model"* — gmail:6865eb93. Goal: expand to higher tiers once value is proven.
- **GTM funnel framework (deck-articulated 4-phase narrative):** Phase 1 *Boutique Wedge* (peace-time status reporting) → Phase 2 *Escalation Hook* (forensic source of truth) → Phase 3 *Network Viral Loop* (vendor mandate) → Phase 4 *De-facto Standard* (shared project assurance layer) — attachment:e7da01c3.

**Sandler-method warning (James, sales coach, 2026-02-24):** *"James cautioned against relying too heavily on free trials, as they may not actually help customers solve their core problems. He suggested Guy be more strategic about how he positions any free offering, and instead focus on getting customers to pay for the value the product provides upfront"* — gmail:1eaea663.

## Conflicts to flag

**1 — *"Strong discovery signal from 60+ conversations"* vs. weak conversion evidence.** Qubit attachment:7218f2cb says *"60+ conversations across IT consulting / SaaS / operators / agencies / construction."* Fathom (~Apr 2026): *"Current Signal: Weak, with customers in IT consultancy, HR, advertising, and construction"* — gmail:11a36f84. Qubit's recommendation (#3): *"Add hard proof around time saved, escalation frequency, CAC assumptions, and willingness to pay"* — i.e. these numbers don't yet exist.

**2 — >50 customers + >$1.2M ARR target vs. $50K ACV assumption.** Deck states the round must prove >50 customers + >$1.2M ARR — that's ~$24K ARR/customer; same deck market-TAM slide assumes $50K ACV. Qubit explicit flag: *"Metrics need reconciliation"* — attachment:ef98142b. **No resolution in indexed data.**

**3 — *"40% of funding dedicated to GTM playbook"* vs. no GTM-budget evidence.** Deck Risks & Mitigations: *"40% of funding is dedicated to optimizing the GTM playbook and cross company expansion"* — attachment:e7da01c3. Indexed reality: no formal budget allocation, no paid GTM headcount, only marketing spend is Cleverly (~$2k/mo at 3-month review). Aspirational deck language with no operational backing.

**4 — ACV pitch variance: $20K vs. $50K.** Lihu's 2026-05-07 intro blurb: *"AskEffi makes that problem go away for $20k/year"* — gmail:abb6e111. Investor deck market model: *"initial blended ACV of $50K"*. Pricing page: $4,000/month = $48K/year for Standard tier (50 projects). The pitch-to-prospect price is materially below the deck-modelled ACV.

**5 — Design-partner paid-status ambiguous.** $500/month design-partner price is described as the entry point; Qubit calls them *"active design partners."* Whether **any** design partner has actually paid $500/month — or whether all are on free pilots — is **not confirmed in any indexed email**.
