# Positioning — what we do

## Current

**One-liner (May 2026 deck headline):** *"Executive Control for Cross-Company IT Projects."*

**Solution-slide framing (May 7, 2026 deck):** *"We're building the system to accelerate executive decisions."*

**Working external blurb (Apr 28, 2026, written by Guy):**

> *Effi is an AI assistant built for executives who oversee complex projects. The problem we solve: when a project hits trouble, senior leaders spend days reconstructing what happened — digging through emails, meeting notes, and threads they weren't copied on — before they can make a single useful decision. That delay is expensive: for a consultancy managing multiple client engagements, a single escalation can cost tens of thousands of dollars in non-billable leadership time, not counting the reputational risk.*
>
> *AskEffi sits on top of your existing communication layer — meetings, emails, documents — and makes it instantly queryable. No process changes, no new workflows for the team. A partner or executive stepping into a problem gets oriented in minutes, not days. And because we manage access at the project level, you control exactly what gets shared — internally, with clients, or with third-party vendors.*
>
> *We're working with IT consultancies and B2B tech companies today, and actively looking for the right partners in the European market.*

**Time-to-context claim:** **2 days → minutes** (formerly framed as "days → 15 minutes"). attachment:e7da01c3-f969-4c67-b44f-efc44780ec99

**End-state vision:** **"Project Assurance Layer"** — the de-facto cross-company collaboration substrate spanning consultancy + client + vendor, mandated by SaaS vendors (SAP, Workday, Oracle) on their partner ecosystems. Phrased in the deck's GTM 4-phase narrative.

## Stable angles (since Nov 2025)

These have not shifted across the entire history:

1. **No new workflows for the team** — Effi gets data as a byproduct of existing emails/meetings/docs. No manual entry.
2. **Access control at the project level** — internal vs external; cross-company safe sharing.
3. **Land in peacetime, prove value before escalation** — the "calm before storm" wedge.
