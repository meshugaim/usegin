> *Pluralitas non est ponenda sine necessitate.*
> "Plurality is not to be posited without necessity."
> — attributed (paraphrase) to William of Ockham, c. 1300s

## The click

Ockham's Razor: when explanations compete and explain the data equally, prefer the one that posits **fewer entities, fewer assumptions, fewer mechanisms**. Not because simple is true — but because every extra entity is another place for the explanation to be wrong, and the simpler one is *cheaper to refute*.

The razor is **not** "always pick the simple thing." It's "don't multiply *without necessity*." When the data demands more, add more. The discipline is in resisting the *unnecessary* addition — the speculative gear, the just-in-case branch, the abstraction that earns its keep only in some imagined future.
