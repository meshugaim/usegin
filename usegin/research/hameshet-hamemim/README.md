# Hameshet HaMemim (חמשת המ"מים) — IDF planning framework

Five-piece operational planning framework taught in IDF officer training. The five Hebrew M-words form the canonical pre-action checklist: *what is the mission, what is the aim, where is the effort concentrated, what is the formation, when does it execute.*

## Purpose

Bring the framework into UseGin doctrine: distill what each M means, map it to what we already do (charters, principle 05, rnd, tikur), and surface the gaps where our practice is fuzzy on a piece IDF officers are sharp on.

## R&D round

Five professors, one per M. Each writes `<m>/whiteboard.md`. Sam cross-cuts in `SYNTHESIS.md` after the five land — preserves disagreements, maps each M to our existing artifacts, names what we should adopt.

The angles:

1. **משימה (mishima) — Mission.** The verb-shaped statement of what the unit must accomplish. Maps to our charter "purpose".
2. **מטרה (matara) — Aim / Objective.** The *because* — the larger purpose this mission serves. Maps to our charter "purpose" / Clausewitz political object.
3. **מאמץ (ma'amatz) — Effort / Schwerpunkt.** Where the main effort goes; what gets the resources; what does not. Maps to principle 6 (Lihu's attention is COG) and Clausewitz's Schwerpunkt.
4. **מערך (ma'arach) — Formation / Array.** Who is positioned where; the structure of forces. Maps to our team shape (rnd's Polls + Sam, cell's spawner + workers, liaison's director + Opus subs).
5. **מועד (mo'ed) — Timing.** When it executes; the temporal coordination piece. Maps weakly to our world (we have battle rhythm gap per C5; D1 mostly).

The hypothesis going in: we are sharp on 1, 2, 3 (charters carry mission + aim + Schwerpunkt). We are *implicit* on 4 (team shapes exist but per-skill, no canonical pattern). We are *weak* on 5 (no battle rhythm at scale, per war research C5).

Sam's job is to test that hypothesis and bring back what's actually load-bearing.
