# Hameshet HaMemim — SYNTHESIS

Cross-cuts the five professor whiteboards (*mishima*, *matara*, *ma'amatz*,
*ma'arach*, *mo'ed*) into one consolidated reading. Audience: Lihu + future
Gins re-orienting. Source whiteboards under `<m>/whiteboard.md`. Read
top-down — convergent rules are the click; divergences are productive
tension; proposed changes are what would land; dilemmas at the bottom need
Lihu before anything moves.

**IDF terms used here, defined inline.** *Mishima* (משימה — mission/task,
verb-of-completion). *Matara* (מטרה — aim, the parent's mission viewed from
below). *Ma'amatz* (מאמץ — main effort / Schwerpunkt). *Ma'arach* (מערך —
formation/array of forces for this mission). *Mo'ed* (מועד — timing/H-hour).
*Pkudat krav* (פקודת קרב — combat order, the artifact a *pkuda* lives in).
*Rosh-gadol* (own the goal, not the words). *Selbständigkeit* (the
*obligation* — not permission — to deviate from the literal task when it
stops serving the purpose).

**Headline.** The five-M decomposition does not hold cleanly at our scale.
*Mishima* and *matara* converge on one shared edit cluster (charter
templates omit the purpose clause). *Ma'amatz* refines principle 05#6 at a
different layer rather than adding new doctrine. *Ma'arach* lands almost
entirely outside the charter skill (the team library is the substrate;
hypothesis was stale at read time). *Mo'ed* proves the absence of the M is
the right answer at our scale (default-empty-but-explicit). The cluster of
four professors hitting "templates silently drop the doctrine" from
different angles is the load-bearing finding of the round.

## 1. TOP — Convergent rules

### CR1. Charter templates silently drop required blocks — single most actionable defect across the round
[mishima + matara + ma'amatz + mo'ed]

The strongest single finding. The `charter` meta-skill correctly demands a
purpose clause (§1) — but every team template under `usegin/teams/<team>.md`
(rnd-team's Poll, tikur-team's Ivan) ships *without* a `## Purpose` slot.
Templates compound: every spawn instantiated from them inherits the
omission. Mishima names this as the *mitala*/mishima bright-line failure
(an action without a purpose clause is a *mitala* — task — not a *mishima*;
the executor owes the action, not the goal). Matara sharpens it: purpose is
*inherited downward* from the parent's mishima, not freely composed —
recursive spawns lose intent at depth ≥2 (the SOD-2006 failure mode in
micro). Ma'amatz finds the parallel cluster (templates omit *priority
designation*). Mo'ed finds the parallel at the timing layer (templates omit
the timing block by accident). Per war-management synthesis C8 (the cluster
is the finding), this is the load-bearing call.

→ Ties to: principle 05#1, principle 05#2, z023, charter §1,
`usegin/teams/*.md`.

### CR2. Mishima and matara are distinct disciplines — collapsing them is the failure mode
[mishima + matara]

*Mishima* is verb-shaped, composed at this echelon, names *what this unit
does* ("seize the bridge by 0600"). *Matara* is state-shaped, **inherited
from one echelon above**, names *what state must hold so the parent's
mission succeeds*. Three doctrinal contrasts: origin (composed vs.
inherited), grammatical shape (verb vs. state), authority for deviation
(matara is what Selbständigkeit answers to — not the local mishima, not the
sub-Gin's preference). Serving the mishima at the cost of the matara is
**failure**, not compliance. Our charter §1 currently reads as if the
spawner authors purpose freely; doctrinally §1 should *quote or point at*
the parent's mishima. Without that, the leaf two echelons down can't
reconstruct Lihu's intent (FM 3-0 two-echelons-down rule).

→ Ties to: principle 05#1, z023, charter §1 + §5.

### CR3. Single verb of completion per mishima — multi-verb is decomposition not done
[mishima + war-management C2 sister convergence]

CALL *Less is Better* + IDF doctrine: a well-formed mishima carries **one**
verb. Multi-verb tasks degrade to *mitala* under fog. Recursive evidence:
the mishima professor's own charter had multi-verb §2 (distill / map /
propose). Ma'amatz, mo'ed, and ma'arach all flagged the same recursive
amusement on their own charters. Meta-skill compensators (Selbständigkeit +
Fresh-Haiku) carried today's charters, but the rule is real — *the first
tell that decomposition isn't done*.

→ Ties to: charter §2, principle 05#1.

### CR4. The team library IS our ma'arach practice — extend by 1-line charter pointer, not by new team files
[ma'arach + (negative-space convergence with mishima/matara/ma'amatz)]

README hypothesis ("we are *implicit* on M4") was **stale at read time**.
`usegin/teams/` already contains 12 named formations with stable casts
(Mark = universal orchestrator in 11/12; Sam = universal synthesizer in
6/12), four canonical coordination patterns, explicit Schwerpunkt-honoring
+ seam-ownership. Doctrinally clean on three of four IDF tests. The real
gap: **the charter at spawn time has no slot for "which formation, and
why"** — future readers can't reconstruct the picking decision. A 1-line
`**Formation.**` block in the charter shape closes this. Lekach: R&D-round
READMEs that predate the substrate they predict about must verify the
substrate before honoring the hypothesis.

→ Ties to: `usegin/teams/`, charter §3.5 (proposed).

### CR5. Default-empty-but-explicit is doctrine — silence-by-accident is the anti-pattern
[mo'ed + mishima + ma'amatz]

Mo'ed's headline: most charters genuinely don't need a timing block, *and
that should be stated, not omitted*. Emit: `**Mo'ed.** None — async; sub-Gin
returns when deliverable lands.` Silence becomes deliberate and defensible
rather than accidental. Generalizes to mishima (templates omitting purpose
by accident) and ma'amatz (rounds omitting ma'amatz designation by
accident). **For any block doctrine names as required-or-explicitly-absent,
the absence must be named in-line, not implied.** Empty + explicit defends;
empty + silent defects.

→ Ties to: charter anti-patterns, principle 05#7, z032.

### CR6. Forbidden inside any M-block: the *how*. Anchors only, never internal tempo
[mishima + matara + ma'amatz + mo'ed]

All four edit-proposing professors named the same forbidden zone. Mishima:
no route, no fire-plan. Matara: state-shaped, not verb-shaped. Ma'amatz:
name the priority, don't dictate how supporting effort sets conditions.
Mo'ed: anchor events other units depend on; never script "spend N minutes
on X" (Modern Application Pitfall 5 — Taylor in McChrystal-land). Across
all five Ms: *anchors-not-script* is the timing/priority/formation-layer
expression of Auftragstaktik §4 ("the *how* is the subordinate's
territory"). The five Ms apply principle 05#1 ("intent before method") at
five different layers.

→ Ties to: principle 05#1, charter §2 anti-patterns.

## 2. MIDDLE — Productive divergences worth keeping

### MD1. Matara/ma'amatz overlap — twin or distinct?

**Matara prof** (Friction #5, named pre-emptively): "doctrinally near-twins
— concentration of purpose vs. concentration of force. Watch for double-
counting." **Ma'amatz prof** (defended): "ma'amatz adds operationally-real
discipline that 05#6 + Clausewitz R5 do not absorb — *per-operation* main-
effort designation across parallel actions, not standing-posture COG
ranking."

**Why preserve.** Both right at different layers. Matara is *what this
unit's mission is for* (parent's purpose, inherited). Ma'amatz is *which
sibling is the main effort within one parent's plan* (priority-of-resource
call at spawn time). Hebrew roots rhyme; operational functions don't.
**Synthesis call:** keep distinct; matara → charter §1 edits; ma'amatz →
rnd skill edits. Do NOT merge proposals just because the roots rhyme.

### MD2. Should this round even be five professors?

Of the five Ms studied: mishima+matara converge on *one* shared edit
cluster (CR1+CR2); ma'amatz refines 05#6 at a different temporal layer (its
real edit lands in `rnd`, not `charter`); ma'arach lands outside the charter
skill (the team library is the substrate); mo'ed produces a *don't-adopt*
default. **A four-M decomposition (purpose-discipline / ma'amatz / ma'arach
/ mo'ed) might be more honest for UseGin.** The IDF keeps five because the
*pkudat krav* template needs five slots; UseGin's charter has one §1 that
absorbs both mishima and matara.

**Why preserve five for this round.** The cluster *is* the finding — five
professors hitting the same template-omission gap from different angles is
stronger evidence than one prof hitting it once. **Recommendation: keep
five for the historical record; mark for re-decomposition next time** (DL2).

### MD3. Ma'amatz designation — load-bearing for rnd, or biases the dialectic?

**Pro.** Polls in an rnd round are peers; the orchestrator should commit,
before firing, to which angle is the main effort. **Counter (steelman by
ma'amatz prof himself).** Polls are intentionally peer-equal; forcing
ma'amatz designation collapses principle 05#7's dialectic.

**Sam's call.** Distinction holds: ma'amatz is a *priority-of-resource-and-
attention* call (this Poll gets Opus; others can be Haiku), not a *priority-
of-truth* call. Synthesizer preserves disagreements regardless. Adopt
ma'amatz in rnd, but Sam's charter must explicitly name "designation is for
resource allocation, not truth weighting" so the synthesizer doesn't read
the designation as a thumb on the scale.

### MD4. Verb-shaped vs. state-shaped purpose — and where it bites

**Mishima** says §1 can be verb- or state-shaped; bar is "single verb of
completion if verb-shaped." **Matara** sharpens to: §1 must be **state-
shaped**; verb-shaped §1 is "matara collapsed into mishima."

**Why preserve.** Layer confusion, not disagreement. Matara's state-shape
rule wins at §1 (which IS matara). Mishima's single-verb rule wins at §2
(key tasks). **Different blocks; both edits land.**

### MD5. Mo'ed proposal (c) vs. war-management DL1 lean B — convergent or in tension?

DL1 lean B: "build `morning-brief`, skip enforced cadence." Mo'ed (c):
default-empty + named exceptions list (tikur of live incident, preflight,
parallel rounds, hook-driven cadence). Both reject (b) hook-driven cadence
at our scale. **Convergent at the structural level; mo'ed (c) strengthens
DL1 lean B by making the rejection defensible at *charter-write time*, not
just at synthesis-write time.**

## 4. Dilemmas to bring Lihu

### DL1. Land the seven charter edits as a single batch, or stage?

**Options.** **A.** Single batch (all 7 + #8) — designed as a system.
**B.** Stage — start with 1+2+3 (highest-load-bearing per CR1), use them
for 5–10 spawns, then layer §3.5 + §6.5. **C.** Run #11 (empirical sample)
*first*; let the data tell us whether the gap is real before any edit
lands.

**Lean: C, then A.** Both mishima and ma'amatz coordinations independently
named "sample first." Risk of A-without-C: editing to fix a gap that
doesn't bite empirically (templates may delegate to spawning skills
successfully in practice). After C confirms, batch (A) for coherence —
edits are append-mostly to existing structure (principle 02), staging
risks Pitfall 6. **Price.** ~one extra session for the sample. **Risk.**
C surfaces "gap less severe than cluster suggests" and proposal reshapes —
that's the dialectic working.

**For you to weigh.** Whether 4-prof convergence is enough evidence on its
own (favors A); whether the empirical pass should be Hebrew-reading
(separate friction); whether to commit *now* to A-after-C-confirms.

### DL2. Re-decompose the M framework for UseGin — collapse to four (or fewer) Ms?

**Options.** **A.** Keep five (preserves IDF framework + this round's
decomposition). **B.** Collapse to four — unify mishima+matara as
"purpose discipline"; keep ma'amatz, ma'arach, mo'ed. **C.** Collapse to
three. **D.** No fixed decomposition — pick Ms per round, list ones that
matter.

**Lean: D for future rounds; A as historical record for this one.**

**Why.** Collapsing post-hoc would erase the recursive evidence (the
cluster wouldn't be visible if ma'amatz and mo'ed weren't both present).
For *next time*, D — let the question drive decomposition rather than
honoring an a-priori list. Generalizes CR7-shaped lekach. **Price.** D
loses a teachable framework; future Gins may re-derive five Ms. The corpus
carries this synthesis as the re-derivation cost. **Risk.** D may be
over-fitting to this round; revisit when the next pre-action-checklist
round fires.

**For you to weigh.** Whether the M framework should remain a teachable
abstraction even if we stop using it as a decomposition rule; whether
matara + ma'amatz should merge as one zettel ("purpose discipline at two
layers") if D ships.

### DL3. Retrospective ma'amatz for this round — recursive lekach

This round had no ma'amatz designation. By proposal #9, it should have.

**Options.** **A.** Mishima — caught the deepest cluster (template-
omission, 4/5 prof generalization). **B.** Mishima + matara as joint —
they converge on the same edit cluster (CR1+CR2). **C.** Mo'ed — most
counterintuitive finding (default-empty is itself doctrine).

**Lean: B (joint mishima+matara).** They are the load-bearing Ms for our
scale; ma'amatz refines an existing principle; ma'arach endorses an
existing substrate; mo'ed proves *not* to adopt the M. The two Polls that
produce *new* charter-skill doctrine are mishima and matara, and they
converge.

**For you to weigh.** Whether retrospective ma'amatz is useful (codifies
lekach for future rounds) or noise (synthesis already weights CR1
highest); whether B is correct or it should be A (single mishima).
